# Feature catalog

These capabilities follow the sequence "webpage -> facts -> evidence -> content -> assets -> publishing packs -> retrospective." Generated content remains draft material, while media is either draft output or output limited by available dependencies. Actual traffic, conversion, and platform performance depend on the real account, platform rules, content quality, and subsequent operations.

| Capability | What it does | Problem it solves | User benefit | Typical use case |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Product webpage reading | Reads the body, title, structured information, and browser snapshot of a public product page, then organizes them into a product fact file | Product information is scattered across a page, so teams can overlook details or treat assumptions as facts | Provides a factual working document with sources, status, and risk notes, reducing avoidable rework | Give it a SaaS product page and first produce a summary of positioning, capabilities, and target users |
| Multi-link and website product discovery | Accepts multiple product links or discovers candidate product pages from a homepage, sitemap, or saved HTML | Copying and screening pages one by one is time-consuming when a product line contains many entries | Builds a candidate list in one run so work can continue according to scores and stated boundaries | Prepare promotion tasks in batches for a website that contains dozens of tool pages |
| Competitor and high-performing content research | Searches public or browser-visible competitors, creators, and high-performing content, then extracts hooks, structures, visible metrics, and sources | Topic selection based only on intuition cannot explain where the reference material came from | Produces a reviewable evidence library, content patterns, and follow-up collection tasks | Before launching an AI tool, compare public content on YouTube, Zhihu, Xiaohongshu, and Douyin |
| Local-login Sidecar research | Uses the separate MediaCrawler Sidecar and local Chrome login state to collect by platform, keyword, detail target, or creator mode | Some platforms expose incomplete public pages, so anonymous reads cannot cover everything visible in a user's browser | Collects more complete browser-visible evidence in a user-controlled local environment while preserving collection status | After signing in locally, collect additional public results for a Xiaohongshu keyword |
| Fact, evidence, and risk controls | Records status for facts, sources, metrics, permissions, and blocked reasons, distinguishing real, public, user-imported, synthetic demonstration, and missing data | Drafts can contain numbers, links, or assumptions that cannot be checked | Shows reviewers what is usable and what still needs evidence | Before publishing, review citations, risk notes, and the `waiting_real_data` status |
| Platform-native copy generation | Creates titles, body copy, tags, and engagement prompts for YouTube, Zhihu, Xiaohongshu, Douyin, GitHub, and other targets | One generic piece of copy does not fit the fields or tone of different platforms | Reduces repetitive rewriting and produces platform-specific drafts ready for review | Prepare a video description, a Zhihu answer, and a Xiaohongshu post for the same product |
| Spoken video scripts and storyboards | Uses product facts and content direction to create spoken scripts, shot order, visual guidance, subtitle emphasis, and asset lists | Copywriting, shooting, and editing lack a shared production structure | Gives creators a script package that can be filmed, reviewed, and handed off | Record a 30–60 second product demonstration and prepare screen captures according to the storyboard |
| MP4 video drafts | When FFmpeg and related dependencies are available, renders scripts, visuals, and audio assets into local MP4 drafts | Text alone does not make pacing or missing visuals easy to inspect | Lets reviewers see duration, aspect ratio, and provisional asset results before formal production | Generate a playable MP4 for the first review of a short-form video |
| Cover and detail images | When Pillow is available, creates PNG cover and detail images required by publishing packs and records their dimensions and paths | Images must otherwise be reorganized for every platform, and files are easy to omit | Delivers images together with the title, video, and publishing fields | Prepare a set of cover and product-detail images for Xiaohongshu or Douyin |
| Complete publishing packs | Collects titles, copy, tags, comment prompts, media paths, tracking links, missing items, warnings, and manual steps | Content, assets, and publishing instructions are scattered across separate folders | Lets a reviewer complete preparation from one checklist while leaving an audit record | Hand an approved task to a creator or client for execution |
| Controlled publishing assistance | Creates dry-run output, official API checks, browser-visible field filling, and publishing queues, then stops before final submission | Publishing mistakes are easy to make, and credential and account-permission boundaries can be unclear | Shows the full payload, screenshots, and missing items before the user decides whether to submit | Run a YouTube official API dry-run or fill visible fields on a Xiaohongshu creator page |
| Post-publication real-evidence import | Imports real URLs, platform exports, screenshots, comments, orders, and revenue files into an evidence inbox | Post-publication data is scattered across platform dashboards, leaving no consistent retrospective entry point | Creates traceable publishing and business evidence without mixing sample numbers into real results | After publishing, add `published-urls.csv`, `metrics.csv`, and comment files |
| Retrospectives and next-iteration optimization | Reads real evidence and reports performance summaries, audience needs, problems, and recommendations for the next topic or hook | Without structured feedback after content is produced, the next iteration relies on guesswork | Uses real data to update content direction and priority | Compare real metrics from different titles before planning the next asset set |
| Turn the current Chrome page into a task | After an explicit user click, reads the current tab URL and title and creates a copyable Skill or assistance command | Manually copying links and parameters while moving from browser to terminal is error-prone | Turns the page being viewed into a local task within seconds while keeping the command reviewable | Open a feature page on the product website and use the extension to create a single-link run command |
| Chinese and English UI | Provides Chinese and English extension interfaces while executing commands and local output according to the selected parameters | Team members who use different languages lack a consistent entry point | Lets Chinese-language operators and English-language collaborators share the same capabilities | A Chinese-language operator selects platforms and an English-language collaborator reviews the generated command |
| Local-first privacy | Keeps output, Cookies, Chrome login profiles, and Sidecar runtime data on the local computer by default | A public release should not carry account state, client data, or hidden tokens | Reduces the surface for data transfer and makes review, backup, and deletion easier | An agency prepares materials on a client's computer and lets the client decide what to share |

## Shared boundaries

- Hosted Worker remains disabled; the public release does not describe hosted execution as available.
- Cookies and Chrome login profiles stay on the local computer and are not uploaded to this public repository or its public release packages.
- Final publishing requires user review and a user-confirmed action; browser-assisted flows stop before final submission.
- The tool does not evade CAPTCHA, platform risk controls, login checks, or account authorization.
- Only real URLs, metrics, comments, orders, and revenue are accepted as real evidence; evidence is not fabricated.
- Payment, subscription, license, credits, and billing backends are excluded only from the feature parity conclusion; the extension's existing billing UI and `billing-contract.json` remain included.
